# Kamakura

An hour away from my dorm in Minamishinagawa is Kamakura, a small town
with a lot of history and culture. The city of Kamakura first became important in 1192 when
the Kamakura Shogunate came to power in Japan and with this Kamakura was named
the capital of Japan for 140 years. The main points of interest in Kamakura
are the many shrines and temples that it has to show, but also tasty food
and the beautiful beach making it a perfect one day trip destination away from
Tokyo.

# The great Buddha of Kamakura

The Kamakura Great Buddha is part of the Kotokuin Temple and is also named one of Japan's national treasures.
At the inside, you can learn about how the statue was constructed about 800 years ago and about
it's turbulent history of being destroyed by natural phenomena such as earthquakes and tsunamis
and the process of it being repaired and the processes of restoration it sustained over the years.

# Tsurugaoka Hachimangu Shrine

Tsurugaoka Hachimangu is Kamakura's most important shrine. It was founded built in 1063,
and enlarged and moved to its current site in 1180 by the founder and first shogun of the
Kamakura government. The shrine houses the Hachiman kami a god for warriors.

A former one thousand year old ginkgo tree near its entrance was uprooted by a storm on 10 March 2010.
The shrine continues to serve as one of the Important Cultural Properties of Japan.
